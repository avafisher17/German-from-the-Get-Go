"Der" Words

Nouns are words assigned to people, places, or things. In English, they often come with "the" pinned to the front.


The baker. The supermarket. The bread.


In German though, you have three different forms of "the"!


Die Bäckerin. Der supermarkt. Das brot.


Don't worry. We'll cover them one at a time!

"Der" = Masculine

Like "dare". Or the beginning of "dairy". And "derière".


In German, nouns are said to be gendered. Nouns of the masculine gender use "der" as their form of "the".


Many random objects will use the masculine gender, but so do people, animals, and other entities that are male or masculine.
